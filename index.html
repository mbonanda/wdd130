Pretoria

The Administrative capital of South Africa, Pretoria, or the 'Jacaranda City' is best known for its annual display of purple flowers in October. Located in the Northern part of Gauteng, Pretoria is the Historic centre of Government, in the former ZAR. Pretoria is also known as Tshwane which is named after; the Tswana/Ndebele Chief Tshwane.

Pretoria stretches along both sides of the Apies River and extends into the Western Hills, of the Magaliesberg on the East. Founded in 1855 by Marthinus Pretorius, son of Andries Pretorius, (the Boer statesman for whom the City was named after). Then becoming: the Capital of the Transvaal in 1860; administrative capital of South Africa in 1910; and a City in 1931. During the South African War (1899–1902), Winston Churchill was imprisoned there in 1899, until his escape.
